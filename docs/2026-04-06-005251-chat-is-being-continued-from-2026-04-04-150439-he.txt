      1079 +\section{Circuit Breaker for External Dependencies}          
      1080 +                                                             
      1081 +\textbf{Challenge:} When the Ollama backend or Redis store ex
           +perienced transient failures such as connection timeouts or s
           +erver restarts, the cache system would stall on each failed r
           +equest, waiting for the full timeout period before falling ba
           +ck. Under sustained failure, every query would incur this tim
           +eout penalty, making the entire system unresponsive even thou
           +gh the cache itself was functioning correctly.               
      1082 +                                                             
      1083 +\textbf{Solution:} A per-dependency circuit breaker was imple
           +mented following the standard three-state pattern. In the CLO
           +SED state, requests flow through normally and failures are co
           +unted. After a configurable failure threshold (default 5 fail
           +ures), the circuit transitions to OPEN and immediately reject
           +s all requests to the failed dependency without waiting, retu
           +rning a fallback response instead. After a recovery timeout (
           +default 30 seconds), the circuit transitions to HALF\_OPEN an
           +d allows a single probe request through. If the probe succeed
           +s, the circuit returns to CLOSED. If it fails, the circuit re

      1087 +\textbf{Challenge:} The Count-Min Sketch used by the W-TinyLF
           +U admission policy accumulates frequency counts indefinitely.

           + are no longer relevant retain high frequency counts, biasing
           + the admission decision in favor of stale entries and against
           + genuinely popular new entries. This frequency staleness beca
           +me apparent during long-running test sessions where the cache
           + population drifted away from the current query distribution.
      1088 +                                                             
      1089 +\textbf{Solution:} A periodic halving mechanism was added to 
           +the Count-Min Sketch. After a configurable number of insertio
           +ns (default equal to the cache capacity), all counters in the
           + sketch are divided by two using integer right shift. This ex
           +ponential decay ensures that recent frequency signals carry m
           +ore weight than old ones, allowing the admission policy to ad
           +apt as the query distribution evolves. The halving threshold 
           +was tuned so that it triggers roughly once per full cache tur
           +nover, balancing responsiveness to distribution shifts agains
           +t the stability of frequency estimates.
